Marsha P. Johnson (1945 - 1992)

"Marsha P. Johnson's name isn't in most American history books, but it should be. Her persistence and advocacy made her one of the most significant figures in LGBTQ activism. Johnson worked as an activist participating in demonstrations and marches as part of the Gay Liberation Front. More than 20 years after her death, Johnson is remembered for her vibrant smile, free spirit and her contributions to the fight for LGBTQ rights (CNN)."

CNN

Renée Richards (1934)

Born Richard Raskind in NYC, she played tennis all through her childhood and becomes one of the best players in the country. In the early 1970's she underwent transitioning surgery and fully transitioned in 1975. She fought for her right to compete as a woman in the US Open. She was the first individual to have sex transition surgery and to compete in a tennis tournament. She retired in 1981 and was inducted in the USTA Eastern Tennis Hall of Fame in 2000 (Wikipedia).

Wikipedia

Paris is Burning (1990)

"Jennie Livingston's monumental documentary of New York's LGBT scene in the 80's, showing the real life of poor Black and Latine LGBT people, introduce the ballrooms, the categories, the houses, the voguing and the dreams and ambitions of these people who are systematically excluded from society. They fight to conquer the right to be and to reinvent themselves in a world starring by straights and white people (IMDB)."
